# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: April 18, 2026*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:**  
SnowballPay is a debt payoff planner that gives people a clear monthly path to become debt-free faster.

**What it does:**  
SnowballPay helps users track debts, choose a payoff strategy (Snowball, Avalanche, or Custom order), and follow a month-by-month plan. It calculates debt-free timelines, interest impact, and strategy tradeoffs, then keeps users on track with progress views, milestones, and planner guidance.

**Product category:**  
Debt payoff planner, personal finance planning app, debt reduction calculator

**Product type:**  
B2C SaaS web app

**Business model:**  
Freemium subscription. Free tier supports core planning with limits (up to 5 debts). Pro tier ($9/month with trial) unlocks unlimited debts, advanced analytics, AI-style recommendations, and deeper planning tools.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:**  
People with multiple debts struggle to prioritize payments and maintain consistency over time.

**Why alternatives fall short:**  
- Spreadsheets are manual and hard to maintain  
- Generic calculators do not support ongoing plan tracking  
- Minimum-payment behavior creates slow, discouraging progress

**What it costs them:**  
Higher lifetime interest, longer payoff windows, repeated decision fatigue, and delayed financial goals.

**Emotional tension:**  
Overwhelm, guilt, anxiety about never catching up, and loss of motivation when progress is not visible.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:**  
Undebt.it, debt payoff calculators, debt planner apps - often less focused on an integrated action workflow (strategy + tracking + guardrails in one place).

**Secondary:**  
Budgeting apps (e.g., YNAB, EveryDollar style workflows) - strong for budgeting, but debt payoff prioritization can be less explicit or less strategy-driven.

**Indirect:**  
Manual spreadsheets, paper budgeting, ad hoc bank app tracking - flexible but high friction and low consistency.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**  
- Strategy flexibility: Snowball, Avalanche, and Custom priority order  
- Planner Intelligence layer: forecast confidence, what-if simulation, guardrails, priority queue, and milestones  
- Month-by-month progress tracking with debt-free timeline visibility  
- Built for decision clarity and consistency, not just one-time calculations

**How we do it differently:**  
SnowballPay combines planning math with execution support so users know both the best strategy and what to do next.

**Why that's better:**  
Users can make faster decisions, avoid strategy drift, and sustain payoff momentum longer.

**Why customers choose us:**  
Simple setup, no bank connection required, clear plan outputs, and visible progress reinforcement.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I can do this in a spreadsheet." | You can, but SnowballPay automates sequencing, recalculations, and timeline impact instantly as inputs change. |
| "I cannot afford another subscription." | Start free and use core planning first; upgrade only if advanced guidance and unlimited debt tracking create enough value. |
| "My income changes too much for fixed plans." | The planner is designed for iterative updates and what-if simulation so the plan can adapt month to month. |

**Anti-persona:**  
Users looking for automated debt settlement, legal debt relief services, or full-service financial advising.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:**  
Current tools are confusing, static, or too manual to keep up with changing debt and income.

**Pull:**  
Clear debt-free date, strategy comparison, and immediate next-action guidance.

**Habit:**  
Existing spreadsheet routines, minimum-payment autopilot behavior, and avoidance of financial dashboards.

**Anxiety:**  
Fear of relying on a new tool, concern about data privacy, and uncertainty about whether the plan will hold up in real life.

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**  
- "I do not know which debt to hit first."  
- "I keep paying but my balances barely move."  
- "I need a plan I can actually stick with."

**How they describe us:**  
- "This finally shows me exactly what to do next."  
- "I can actually see my debt-free date."  
- "It makes debt payoff feel manageable."

**Words to use:**  
clear plan, debt-free date, payoff order, momentum, track progress, interest saved, realistic, flexible

**Words to avoid:**  
guaranteed results, instant debt elimination, get rich quick, shame-based language

**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Snowball | Pay smallest balances first to build momentum |
| Avalanche | Pay highest APR debts first to minimize total interest |
| Custom Priority | User-defined debt order based on personal strategy |
| Acceleration | Extra payment above minimums each month |
| Guardrail Buffer | Cash left after planned debt payments to reduce risk |
